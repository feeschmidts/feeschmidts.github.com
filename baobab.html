How to give everyone a voice in a participatory art project?

Under the Baobab Website

A website to accompany the Baobab tree revealing stories and inspirations behind fabrics.

The tree is produced by Pirate Technics, made from fabrics selected and designed by Masters textile students from Chelsea Collage of Art and Design. The tree was exhibited as part of the Festival of the world throughout summer 2012, representing the spirit of how the power of art can bring people together and change lives.

Building website

The Baobab website I wrote single handedly in html5 and css3, integrating jquery and javascript elements such as fancybox v2 and Soundcloud Strata. The site is hosted for free on Staticloud

The site was tested in Firefox, Ie8, Opera, Chrome, Safari and on the Iphone & Ipad

Exhibition

The 'Fabrics of the World website' is exhibited in the museum part of the Festival of the World, ground floor Southbank Centre amonst the background exhibitions of the other projects.

Learning & Improvement

Automation

Incorporating the work of 30 students, it would have been great to design an automated process to summon captions and descriptions for the assets from the students directly

Exhibition Parental setting

As all resources were sourced from external apis, I was disappointed to see that no one at the southbank centre had tested the site for functionality and setting parental settings correctly.
